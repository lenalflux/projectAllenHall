A list of favorite resources... so far!

Khan Academy
An amazing resource with FREE education as its mission. Sal Khan provides everything you need to brush up / start on your math journey. There is also a computer programming section which I used to get started on this journey.

Teach yourself Computer Science
This extensive guide dives deep into the subject of computer science and what it takes for one to take the challenge on themselves. It illustrates a long but clear path into the field. I have'nt quite caught up with even the beginning of this guide but it will be go to throughout my learning journey.

Reddits Learning Programming Getting Started Wiki
Another great guide for the even more beginner friendly (me) needs. This comes with a few videos, resources, book lists, and a community (most important) that will help me get started on the way. Its great to ask yourself 'Why do I want to program?' when getting started. Set goals early and started working on projects.

Programming with Mosh
So this is a PAID resource. It is about $35 per month. I decided to go with this particular programming because I really enjoy the way he speaks, articulates, and he obviously has a very commanding understanding of the knowledge necessary to program. I spend many hours on youtube and using FREE resources before I decided to go with a paid instructor. To be clear I probably spent around 200 hours of learning before I decided to get this subscription. If you are thinking about the same route I suggest you exhaust as many free resources as possible until you feel the need to pay someone. It is really nice (to me) to have well laid out structures and progress indicators. It helps small goals and feel like I have been making progress.
